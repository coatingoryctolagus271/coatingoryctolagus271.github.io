## 🚀 Getting started

When you open the application, you see the project dashboard. This area acts as the main hub for your workflow. 

### Setting up a project
To start a new project, select File and then New Project. Give your project a name and choose the folder where you want to save your work. 

### Creating screen captures
Select the Record button to begin capturing your screen. You can choose to record your full desktop or a specific window. The software captures your audio and video in sync. Press F10 on your keyboard to stop the recording. The file saves automatically to your project folder.

### Adding callouts
Callouts help your viewers focus on specific parts of your video. Open the Callouts tab and drag your chosen shape onto the timeline. You can adjust the timing and look of the shape. Use callouts to highlight buttons, menus, or text on the screen.

### Building quizzes
Quizzes allow you to test your viewers. Go to the Interactive section and select Add Quiz. Insert your questions at the timeline points where you want them to appear. You can set markers that stop the video until the viewer selects the correct answer. This feature proves useful for training modules.

## 🛠️ Using export presets

Consistent video quality helps your audience. Instead of choosing settings every time you save a video, use the export presets. 

Go to the Export menu and select Manage Presets. Here you create new settings for different platforms. For example, make a preset for high-quality YouTube uploads and another for quick internal training files. When you finish your video, select the preset and the software handles the rest.

## 💡 Troubleshooting common issues

If you face problems during use, try the following steps.

*   **Application does not open:** Ensure you have the latest version of the .NET framework installed on your computer.
*   **Audio out of sync:** Close background programs that use your microphone or speakers.
*   **Export takes too long:** Check that you have enough available storage space on your hard drive. 
*   **Missing video files:** Use the search function in the Project Manager to locate files moved from their original folder.

## 📁 Project structure

The application organizes your files to keep your computer tidy. Every project contains subfolders for your media:
*   /RawCaptures: Stores your original, unedited screen recordings.
*   /Assets: Contains your callouts, quiz data, and images.
*   /Exports: Holds your final, completed video files.

By keeping these folders in one place, you prevent broken links within your project. Always keep your project folder intact if you move it to a different drive or cloud storage.
